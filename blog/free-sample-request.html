Client Asking for Free Sample? Here’s What to Do

Free sample requests are one of the biggest hidden profit killers in freelancing.

At some point, every freelancer hears this: “Can you do a small sample before we proceed?”

It sounds harmless. But over time, this behavior drains hours of unpaid work.

Why clients ask for free samples

  • They want to reduce risk
  • They don’t trust portfolios
  • They are comparing multiple freelancers
  • Or they are trying to get free work

The hidden problem

Free samples shift risk entirely onto you.

  • You invest time without guarantee
  • Client has no commitment
  • Work can be reused without payment

Better alternatives

1. Show past work

Your portfolio exists to eliminate the need for free samples.

2. Offer paid trial

A small paid task filters serious clients immediately.

3. Limit scope strictly

If you must do a sample, keep it minimal and clearly defined.

Real scenario

A freelancer completes a “small test task.” The client disappears or uses the work without hiring. This is extremely common.

Better workflow

Charge first → Deliver controlled preview → Unlock full work after payment

Core rule

No payment = no deliverable work

Stop doing unpaid work.
